Recovered chronology

The outbreak was not an accident. It was a deadline.

Mortigen Biopharma sold a promise of youth without decay. Behind the branding sat a private annex, investor pressure, and a beta release that detonated into a mass-casualty event.

Phase 01

Private longevity lab

Mortigen Biopharma built an isolated annex for regenerative medicine and accelerated tissue repair, aiming to sell youth as a premium product.

Phase 02

Investor pressure beats caution

Staff objections were ignored. The restricted human beta went live before long-term neural stability was understood, and the facility fell within minutes.

Phase 03

Last survivor inside

Dr. Luisen sealed himself inside an armed research block, weaponized whatever remained in the lab, and stayed alive long enough to expose what Mortigen released.

Threat index

The annex keeps changing, but the pressure only goes up.

Swarm pressure

Walkers and fast infected

The run opens with direct pursuers, then layers in zigzag and fast enemies to punish lazy routes and weak spacing.

  • Predictive spawning pushes enemies into your path.
  • Regular spawn intervals shrink as waves advance.
  • Full unlock mix reaches four standard enemy behaviors.
Spacing breakers

  • Vehicle runs change how you survive contact pressure.

Privacy by design

No account wall. No backend. No hidden analytics layer.

Mortigen stores gameplay progression locally on-device. Rewarded ads and payments are handled through platform services only when the player explicitly uses them.

Local-only progression

High score, survival time, trophies, audio settings, store ownership, and selected outfit are persisted locally with no server-side profile.

No account or analytics SDK

No login, no email capture, no location collection, and no remote analytics system are described in the current product architecture.

Optional platform monetization

Rewarded revive ads use Google AdMob on mobile, and purchases are processed through Google Play Billing or Apple In-App Purchase.
